About

When I discovered the Design Thinking Process, I realized that my love for creation could be used as an instrument to help others. Now at Northwestern's McCormick School of Engineering, I look to turn my ideas into better, easier, and more vibrant realities.

2024
NYU Medication Non-Adherence Mock Solutions
In 2024, I participated in a summer program at the NYU Tandon School of Engineering called Design, Invent, and Innovate. My team was tasked with finding a potential solution to elderly people not taking their pills as prescribed (medication non-adherence). Throughout the two week program we were able to conduct a rapid design process: we asked elders in our own lives how they handle their prescriptions, narrowed down our problem statement, generated three potential solutions and then created a presentation explaining our process. We weren't able to create a final product given the expedited time line, but it still was an incredible learning experience. This was my first design project with defined time and budget constraints, forcing me to adapt my processes and challenge myself to work faster without sacrificing quality.
2025
The Windup Toy
In my junior year of high school I started an independent study with my design teacher. After working at a bike shop all summer, I was interested in gear mechanics and how they work. We didn't have the resources to build a bike, so I began designing a machine with similar properties: the windup toy. I wanted my windup toy to function as a teaching device for other students interested in gears, so I turned the structure inside-out, allowing the user to see what was happening while it was running. As it was wound, the user could see the bands tightening and the gears turning. Using ball-bearings, basswood, Adobe Illustrator, a 3D printer, and a laser cutter, I went through numerous mockups before reaching a third prototype. All the while, the bike served as a guide for my design: I tried to make it fun to use, put an emphasis on visual appeal, and focused on the user experience.
2026
Linen Cart Enclosure
In Northwestern Engineering's signature freshman class, Design Thinking and Communication, students apply a human-centered design process to a real world problem. In this course my group was partnered with the Shirley Ryan Ability Labs, a rehab center in Chicago whose nurses were forgetting to close the velcro straps on their linen carts. This small, seemingly mundane issue actually causes potential health code violations as the linens need to be fully secured when not in use. Throughout the 10 week course my group was able to interview stakeholders / users, prototype mockups, test and iterate those solutions, before ultimately presenting our final design at the DTC expo.

Sneaker Design
As a basketball player, I've always been obsessed with my footwear. What shoes will maximize my performance? What shoes will make me look the best? Why? During quarantine, I fell in love with the work of Tinker Hatfield, the man who designed Michael Jordan's signature sneaker line and many other famous silhouettes. Inspired by his work and the sketches of other designers like Virgil Abloh and Benjamin Nethongkome, I began creating my own shoes, each with their own story and purpose.

Graffiti — STAK

Works

As a visual thinker growing up in New York City I couldn't help but be drawn to graffiti. After watching the 1983 film "Style Wars" in middle school and seeing kids my age throwing up cool pieces, I realized that I too could play a role in the artform. I started sketching in my notebook, developing my own personal handstyle under the name STAK. STAK is a representation of my last name "Naparstek", but it also symbolizes my artistic process: stacking days, being consistent, and building upon what came before me. Works are inspired by Skeme, Dondi, Kase2, Seen, Donald Judd, and Georgia O'Keefe.
